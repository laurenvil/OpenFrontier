Biology Study Notes - Cell Biology

Mitochondria is the powerhouse of the cell. It generates ATP (adenosine triphosphate) through cellular respiration. The process involves breaking down glucose in the presence of oxygen.

Photosynthesis requires sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide. It occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells. The chemical equation is: 6CO2 + 6H2O + light -> C6H12O6 + 6O2. Plants convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose.

DNA stands for deoxyribonucleic acid. It carries the genetic instructions for the development and function of living organisms. DNA has a double helix structure, discovered by Watson and Crick in 1953. The four bases are adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), and cytosine (C).

The cell membrane is a phospholipid bilayer that controls what enters and exits the cell. It is selectively permeable, meaning it allows some molecules through while blocking others. This is crucial for maintaining homeostasis.

Mitosis is the process of cell division that results in two identical daughter cells. It consists of four stages: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase. Mitosis is essential for growth and repair of tissues.
