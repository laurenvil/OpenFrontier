The Industrial Revolution began in Great Britain in the late 18th century. It marked a shift from hand-production methods to machines, new chemical manufacturing, and iron production processes. Coal became the primary fuel source.

Key inventions included the spinning jenny (1764), the steam engine improvements by James Watt (1769), and the power loom (1785). These inventions transformed textile manufacturing and transportation.

The Industrial Revolution led to urbanization as workers moved from rural farms to cities for factory jobs. Working conditions were often harsh, with long hours, low pay, and child labor being common.
